Dataset

Planetary, stellar, and derived features used for habitability analysis

Planetary Parameters

Physical properties of the exoplanets in the dataset

🌏

Planet Size

Measured by comparing it to Earth. Knowing how big a planet is helps us guess whether it is a rocky world like ours, or a massive gas giant like Jupiter.

Unit: Earth Radii (R⊕)
⚖️

Planet Weight (Mass)

Measured by comparing how heavy it is compared to Earth. A planet needs enough weight (gravity) to hold onto an atmosphere, which is essential for breathing and staying warm.

Unit: Earth Masses (M⊕)
⏱️

Year Length (Orbit)

The time it takes for the planet to travel all the way around its sun. If a year is very short, the planet is probably extremely close to the star and burning hot.

Unit: Earth Days
📏

Distance from Sun

How far the planet is from its star. This is the biggest factor in deciding if the planet is in the "Habitable Zone" where it’s not too hot and not too cold for water.

Unit: Astronomical Units (AU)

Stellar Parameters

Properties of the host stars that influence planetary habitability

🌟

Stellar Temperature

Effective surface temperature of the host star measured in Kelvin. Determines the spectral type and the habitable zone boundaries for orbiting planets.

Unit: Kelvin (K)
💡

Stellar Luminosity

Total energy output of the star relative to the Sun (L☉). Higher luminosity pushes the habitable zone further from the star.

Unit: L☉
☀️

Stellar Radius

Physical size of the host star measured in solar radii (R☉). Affects the transit depth and the extent of the star's gravitational influence on orbiting planets.

Unit: R☉

Derived Features

Calculated parameters that provide deeper habitability indicators

☢️

Insolation Flux

The amount of stellar radiation received by the planet, measured relative to Earth (S⊕). This is one of the most critical indicators for habitability as it directly determines the energy available for maintaining liquid water on the surface.

Earth-like range: 0.36 — 1.11 S⊕

Unit: S⊕ (Earth flux)
🌡️

Equilibrium Temperature

The theoretical surface temperature of the planet assuming no atmosphere, calculated from stellar luminosity and orbital distance. Provides a baseline for estimating actual surface conditions.

Habitable range: 180 — 310 K

Unit: Kelvin (K)
