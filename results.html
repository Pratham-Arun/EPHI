Key Findings

Summary of the most significant results from the analysis

🏆

XGBoost Leads

XGBoost achieved the highest overall accuracy at 94.2%, with particularly strong performance in identifying potentially habitable planets. Its gradient boosting approach effectively captured complex feature interactions.

🔑

Insolation Flux Dominates

Insolation flux emerged as the most important feature for habitability prediction, accounting for approximately 28% of model decision power. This aligns with astrophysical understanding of habitable zone dynamics.

📊

Class Imbalance Impact

Only 12% of the dataset represents potentially habitable planets. SMOTE oversampling increased recall for the habitable class from 67% to 89%, significantly reducing the number of missed habitable candidates.
